This book-length manuscript argues that AI governance should not be understood only as compliance, risk management, or procedural reassurance. It studies the trap as a structural form: the way institutions prearrange harm, distribute burden, and make some forms of challenge harder to enact in time.

The public entry keeps the frame concise. What matters here is the governing claim: systems should be designed so classifications can be reconstructed, thresholds can be contested, and stop conditions can interrupt harm before it hardens into routine procedure.
