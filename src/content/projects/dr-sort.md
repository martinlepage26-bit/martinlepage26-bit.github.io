*Dr.Sort* is the corpus-triage line inside the broader archive-tool family. It focuses on intake, batching, sorting windows, and the practical handling of document sets when the archive stops being manageable by memory alone.

The current surface treats sorting as a visible queue with explicit reservations, priorities, and handoff points. That makes the work legible: what is waiting, what is being handled now, and where accountability sits for the next pass.

Its closest companion is *SCRIPTO*: *Dr.Sort* organizes the corpus and the review windows, while *SCRIPTO* turns a bounded packet into a manuscript-facing output.
