This lecture was part of Cégep du Vieux Montréal's Awareness Week on Sexual Diversity and Gender Equality. It examined contemporary paganism through the lens of queer studies and sociology of religion, exploring how neo-pagan communities — particularly in Montreal — serve as sites where 2SLGBTQ+ practitioners negotiate gender norms, construct authentic identities, and develop ritual forms outside the constraints of more institutionalized religious traditions.

The presentation draws on over a decade of ethnographic and theoretical engagement with Montreal's pagan community, including fieldwork conducted for doctoral research at UQAM and subsequent published work on queer ritualities, gender performativity, and the question of authenticity in Wicca and related practices.
