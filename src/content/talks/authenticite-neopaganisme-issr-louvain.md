This presentation was delivered at the International Society for the Sociology of Religion (ISSR) 33rd Conference, held at UCLouvain in Louvain-la-Neuve, Belgium in July 2015. The conference theme was "Sensing Religion."

The paper examined how queer practitioners within Montreal's neo-pagan and Wiccan communities construct and claim authentic religious identities. In traditions that privilege personal experience and individual meaning-making over doctrinal authority, authenticity emerges as a site of negotiation rather than a given — shaped by gender performance, community belonging, and the tension between inherited symbolic systems and queer self-definition.

An expanded version was published as "Religiosités queer néo-païennes et la question de l'authenticité dans la Wicca" in *Religiologiques*, vol. 36, Spring 2018, pp. 195–221.
