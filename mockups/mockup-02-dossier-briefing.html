Framing / About

Without boundary discipline, procedural fluency can be mistaken for control.

The critical risk is interpretive slippage: categories retain legitimacy signals even when the evidence, authority, and implementation conditions that once supported them have changed.

Governance failure often appears first as a language mismatch before it appears as a system incident.

Contradiction register

  • System outputs are described as objective while category boundaries remain negotiated.
  • Accountability is described as distributed while intervention authority remains concentrated.
  • Risk policies claim neutrality while burden patterns indicate uneven institutional exposure.

Governance / Advisory

Intervention options

Option Mechanism Expected effect
Boundary tagging Classify all governance claims as verified, inferred, or unresolved. Reduces certainty inflation in leadership summaries.
Ownership hardening Assign named and fallback owners at each decision node. Prevents accountability gaps during escalation events.
Relational review lane Jointly review system outputs, policy framing, and social consequence. Surfaces hidden mismatch before public failure.
